Pycnonotus jocosus fuscicaudatus

Distribution in the Indian peninsula, western region and central India. The cover is almost complete and the white part of the throat (neck) is clear, not white in the tail at the tail. (more white tail).

View

Pycnonotus jocosus abuensis

Northwestern India (west of Rajasthan province, north of Maharashtra, but absent in many dry areas). A light-colored bib, which looks like it's cut off by segments and the tail is white.

View

Pycnonotus jocosus pyrrhotis

North India (from east Punjab to Arunachal Pradesh) and Nepal. There is a light back, complete cover like Pycnonotus subspecies jocosus fuscicaudatus and white tail feathers are very clear.
